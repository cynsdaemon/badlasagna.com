Comic 001 took me a little longer than planned — mostly because I missed my own deadline while obsessing over Harriett’s design. I spent way too much time drawing her, redrawing her, and then redrawing her again. Perfection really tried to hijack the whole process, but I’m learning to prioritize getting it done over getting it flawless.

A big part of the slowdown was learning Procreate on the fly. Brushes, layer effects, blend modes…It’s a lot. I kept bouncing between experimenting with tools and sketching Harriett in my notebook. I’ll share some of those early sketches soon — she went through a few "identity crises" before landing on her current grey‑skinned palette, which I actually love. 

![Harriett early sketches](images\001_Harriett_Turnaround.jpg) 

Even with the challenges, there were bright spots. Drawing the Gingerbread Man was ridiculously fun, and once I settled into Procreate’s workflow, creating simple comic panels felt surprisingly smooth. My iPad Mini 6 and Apple Pencil 2 handled everything like champs, and my custom Bad Lasagna color palette made the whole thing feel more cohesive.

Now that the panel is out, I’m asking myself a few questions: 

- Did the panels convey the idea clearly? 
- What would I change next time? 

For starters, I want to simplify Harriett’s shapes and lines. She’s cute, but she’s a little over‑designed for a recurring character. And honestly, I’m trying not to get too attached — her look will evolve as I get more comfortable with the style.

Tools used: Procreate, Bad Lasagna custom palette, iPad Mini 6.

Starting is always the hardest part, but I’m proud of this one.
